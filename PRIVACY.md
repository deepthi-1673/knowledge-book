# Privacy Policy — Knowledge Book

_Last updated: 2026-07-13_

Knowledge Book is a browser extension that lets you save highlighted text from web
pages into notes stored on your own device, with optional AI summaries and export to
Obsidian.

## What data the extension handles

- **Your notes and settings** (the text you choose to save, page titles, source URLs,
  tags, page/notebook names, your model choice, and your API key) are stored **locally
  on your device** using the browser's extension storage (`chrome.storage.local`).
- This data is **never sent to the developer** and there are **no analytics, no
  tracking, and no advertising** of any kind.

## Data sent to third parties (only when you choose to)

- **OpenRouter (openrouter.ai):** If you turn on AI summaries in Settings, the text you
  save and any custom instructions you set are sent to OpenRouter using **your own API
  key**, solely to generate a summary. This is optional and off by default. Your use of
  OpenRouter is governed by OpenRouter's privacy policy.
- **Obsidian (on your device):** If you click "Send to Obsidian", the note content is
  passed to the Obsidian desktop application on your computer via an `obsidian://` link.
  Nothing is uploaded to any server in this step.

Your API key is stored locally and is transmitted only to OpenRouter. It is never sent
to the developer or any other party.

## Permissions and why they are needed

- **Access to websites (host access):** so you can highlight and save text on any page
  you visit. The extension only reads page content when you explicitly save a selection.
- **Storage:** to keep your notes and settings on your device.
- **Context menu:** to add the "Save selection to Knowledge Book" right-click item.
- **Notifications:** to tell you if a save fails.

## Data retention and deletion

All data lives on your device. You can delete individual notes in the book, or remove
everything by clearing the extension's data or uninstalling the extension.
